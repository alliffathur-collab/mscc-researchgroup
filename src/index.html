Quick research areas

Core areas

The group studies computational catalysis, in-silico materials design for energy, and vibrational spectroanalytical investigations of bioactive compounds for reliable interpretation and ultra-sensitive detection.

Molecular Infrared & Raman Spectroscopies

Vibrational spectroscopic analysis using IR and Raman to probe molecular structure.

Computational Chemistry

Electronic-structure methods and DFT to predict reactivity and properties.

In-Silico Materials Design for Energy & Catalysis

Design and screening of materials for energy storage and catalytic applications.

Rational design & structure–property relationships

We pursue rational materials design by linking atomic-scale structure with observed properties to guide synthesis and experimental validation.

Computational catalysis

Mechanisms, adsorption, and charge-transfer processes studied using established electronic-structure tools.

  • Gaussian
  • VASP
  • CASTEP
  • D-Mol3
  • Quantum ESPRESSO
  • others

Metal-based and metal-free systems; 0D–3D materials; single- to multi-atom decorated structures; gas adsorption for toxic gases and environmental pollutants.

Theory–spectroscopy integration

Combining theory and vibrational spectroscopy (IR, Raman, SERS, EC-SERS) for spectral assignment and interpretation of molecular interactions with nanostructures.

Integrated theory + spectroscopy

Integrated theory and spectroscopy for materials development and spectral interpretation.
